About Me

I am a Cambridge-track high-school student in Hanoi who likes turning everyday objects into measurable, connected systems. My favorite kind of problem starts with a physical signal — heat, dust, moisture, sound — and ends with a decision a person can actually act on.

That path led me to build a non-invasive sleep-monitoring pillow that estimates body temperature with fuzzy logic, and Air Map, a real-time air-quality network for Hanoi whose data lights up a physical LED map of the city.

Along the way I completed MIT's Introduction to Computer Science using Python, competed at the VEX IQ Robotics national championship, and won a Bronze Medal at Vietnam AI Champion (Hanoi University of Science & Technology).

Off the workbench, I have played piano for ten years, teach English to younger students, and volunteer with community charity drives — because technology is only useful if it reaches people.

PRJ-001

Smart Pillow — Sleep-Monitoring System

Working prototype

A non-invasive pillow that observes sleep through embedded sensors — no wearables, no straps — and estimates core body temperature with a fuzzy-logic model.

Three temperature sensors and a humidity sensor are distributed inside the pillow body. A temperature gradient across the sensors reveals head position; humidity trends indicate night sweating. Data streams over BLE to a gateway and on to a cloud dashboard with trends and alerts for families, schools and elderly-care programs.

PRJ-002

Air Map — Real-Time Air-Quality Ecosystem

1I1W 2025 entry

An IoT network that makes Hanoi's invisible air pollution tangible — a physical LED map of the city glows green, yellow or red district by district, in real time.

Custom ESP32 sensing nodes measure PM2.5/PM10 dust plus temperature and humidity, and push readings over Wi-Fi to the ThingSpeak cloud. A central controller pulls the data back via REST API to drive addressable LEDs embedded in a laser-cut 3D map, while a companion mobile app (MIT App Inventor) shows AQI charts and history. Presented at the 1I1W 2025 International Innovation, Design & Startup Competition.
